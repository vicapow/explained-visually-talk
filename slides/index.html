What are explorable explanations?
They make complex problems or ideas easier to grasp by allowing you to play with them.
# Readers can... + Ask questions + Consider alternatives + Question the assumptions of the author
+ They allow for active readers. You can't ask a book a question. + This is the facility a teacher traditionally provides. + In the cases where a teacher isn't available, it can be extremely valuable.
__Active__ readers are more focused and engaged with the content.
+ I've never fallen asleep talking to someone. I can't say the same for reading. + It's easier to stay interested and engaged with content you can interact with.
__Simulation__ to make the abstract concrete. + [Central Limit](http://blog.vctr.me/posts/central-limit-theorem.html) + [Conditional probability](http://setosa.io/ev/conditional-probability/) + [Markov Chains](http://setosa.io/ev/markov-chains/)
Simulation can be a powerful tool for explaining difficult, or abstract concepts by making them concrete.
__Interactivity__ to connect concepts. + [Digital filter](http://worrydream.com/ExplorableExplanations/#explorableExample) + [Sine and Cosine](http://setosa.io/ev/sine-and-cosine/) + [PCA](http://setosa.io/ev/principal-component-analysis/)
Interactivity can help take a concrete example and make it abstract.
Engaging content that's __fun__ yet doesn't distract from the main point. + [Parable of the polygons](http://ncase.me/polygons/) + [Image kernels](http://setosa.io/ev/image-kernels/)
If something is fun and engaging, doing or using it doesn't seem like a chore. Exploratorium is the extreme of this. They're explorable explanations where the explanation is optional. Their goal is get you excited and motivated about a concept enough for you to want to learn more about it on your own.
